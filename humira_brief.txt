COMPETITIVE MARKETING BRIEF: HUMIRA
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. DRUG OVERVIEW
   - Generic name: Adalimumab, Drug class: Biologic
   - Approved indication(s): Rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, Crohn’s disease, ulcerative colitis, ankylosing spondylitis, and plaque psoriasis
   - How it works: Humira works by blocking a specific protein in the body that causes inflammation, helping to reduce symptoms of autoimmune diseases.
   - FDA approval status: Initially approved, with ongoing updates and expansions of indications

2. MANUFACTURER & COMMERCIAL POSITION
   - Which company makes it: AbbVie Inc.
   - Any revenue or market share signals found in the research: As of 2024, Humira dominated the market with 87.0% share, but its sales have been steadily declining due to biosimilar competition, with a 3% drop in sales in 2024.
   - How long it has been on the market: Humira has been on the market since its initial approval, with a long-standing presence and strong brand recognition.

3. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
   Identify the top 2-3 competing drugs. For each competitor write:
   - Drug name + company: Amjevita (Amgen), Hyrimoz (Sandoz), Cyltezo (Boehringer Ingelheim)
   - Key differentiator vs Humira: Biosimilars offering similar efficacy at a lower cost
   - Who it is winning with: Payors, such as CVS Caremark, which has removed Humira from its national preferred formularies in favor of biosimilars, and patients seeking more affordable treatment options.
